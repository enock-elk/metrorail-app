Commuter Guide & FAQ

Everything you need to know about how Metrorail Next Train routes your trips, processes data, and keeps you moving.

👋🏾

Built by Commuters, for Commuters

Metrorail Next Train is an independent, unofficial tool. We are not affiliated with PRASA or Metrorail. This app was built because commuters deserve clear, accessible, and digital transit information.

Currently, the app relies on crowdsourced schedules and official PRASA timetable releases. The app tells you where the train should be based on the schedule, not its live GPS location. While true live-tracking is our ultimate architectural goal, for now, we rely on the community to report delays and missing routes.

🧠

Understanding the Algorithm

1. Directional Naming (e.g., "Train to Kempton Park")

When the app says "Next train to Kempton Park," it is indicating the direction the train is heading, based on its final destination. If you are traveling from Pretoria to Centurion, you will board the "Train to Kempton Park" because Centurion is a stop along that specific corridor.

2. Why are some trains hidden? (Smart Filtering)

Our routing engine is designed to find you the fastest path home. We use a mathematical filter (Pareto dominance) to drop inferior train options from your results.

Example: If Train A requires two transfers and takes 3 hours, but Train B leaves slightly later, is direct, and gets you there in 1 hour, the engine will hide Train A. It assumes you would rather wait on the platform for a faster, simpler ride than spend hours bouncing between stations.

⏱️

Transfers & The 4-Hour Rule

We actively calculate multi-leg journeys across shared corridors (e.g., transferring at Germiston or Koedoespoort). However, we enforce a strict 4-Hour Maximum Layover limit.

If a trip requires you to wait at a transfer hub for more than 4 hours for your connecting train, the engine considers the timetable "too sparse" and will declare the route mathematically impossible for that day. This often happens on weekends when service is drastically reduced.

How to Install Next Train

Next Train is currently a lightweight "Web App" that installs directly from your browser, saving your storage space!

For iPhone (iOS) Users

Open the app in Safari. Tap the Share button at the bottom of the screen (the square with an arrow pointing up), scroll down, and tap "Add to Home Screen".

For Android Users

If you are using a supported browser like Chrome, a green "Install App in The Background" button will appear at the bottom of the main menu. Simply tap it! If you don't see the button, your current browser might not support web installations.

Coming Soon to App Stores

We are working hard to bring Next Train natively to both the Google Play Store and the Apple App Store in the near future!

Frequently Asked Questions

Does this app work offline?

Yes! Once you open the app while connected to the internet, it saves the schedules to your device. You can check times while underground or during loadshedding without using data.

Are you expanding to other provinces?

Absolutely. We are currently actively mapping out the corridors for KwaZulu-Natal (KZN) and the Eastern Cape (EC). We track demand, so be sure to vote for your region in the App Hub!

How can I help improve the app?

As a crowdsourced tool, we rely on commuters like you. If you notice a missing train, or if you have a photo of a new timetable at your local station, please use the Feedback button in the app to send it to us so we can update the database.

Why is my route showing "No Physical Connection"?

If the Trip Planner fails to find a route, it means our system currently doesn't have a known transfer hub connecting your Origin and Destination. If you know a valid transfer route, please hit the "Report Missing Route" button to let us know!
